Nikola Tesla

Inventor, Electrical Engineer, and Futurist

Nikola Tesla portrait, 1890

Here's a brief timeline of Nikola Tesla's life:

  • 1856 - Born on July 10 in Smiljan, Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia).
  • 1875 - Enrolled at the Austrian Polytechnic in Graz to study electrical engineering.
  • 1881 - Started working for the Continental Edison Company in France.
  • 1884 - Arrived in New York City and started working for Thomas Edison.
  • 1887 - Developed the induction motor and AC power transmission system.
  • 1891 - Constructed the Tesla coil, a high-voltage resonant transformer circuit.
  • 1893 - Powering the 1893 World's Columbian Exposition in Chicago, Tesla demonstrated the feasibility and safety of transmitting electrical power without wires.
  • 1896 - Conducted experiments in Colorado Springs, exploring wireless power transmission and investigating atmospheric electricity.
  • 1901 - Began construction of the Wardenclyffe Tower, intended to be a wireless communication and power transmission station.
  • 1943 - Passed away on January 7 in New York City at the age of 86.

Contributions and Innovations:

  • Tesla's development of the alternating current (AC) power system revolutionized the generation, transmission, and distribution of electricity.
  • His invention of the Tesla coil paved the way for the development of wireless technologies and high-frequency electrical phenomena.
  • Tesla's work on wireless power transmission laid the foundation for modern wireless communication and power transfer systems.
  • He made significant contributions to the fields of robotics, electric power, and magnetism.
  • Tesla's visionary ideas and concepts, such as wireless energy, electric vehicles, and renewable energy, continue to inspire scientists and inventors today.

Quotes by Nikola Tesla:

"The day science begins to study non-physical phenomena, it will make more progress in one decade than in all the previous centuries of its existence."

"The present is theirs; the future, for which I really worked, is mine."
